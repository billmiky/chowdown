title: "Spicy Skillet Ground Turkey & Snap Peas"

ingredients:
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 red onion, halved and thinly sliced into half-moons
- 1 lb ground turkey
- 1 lb sugar snap peas, trimmed
- Salt, as needed
- 2 tbsp chopped roasted cashews or peanuts (optional)
- 1/4 cup fresh lime juice (from 2–3 limes), plus more to taste
- 2 tbsp fish sauce (sub soy sauce or coconut aminos), plus more to taste
- 1/2 tsp red-pepper flakes
- 1/2 cup torn mint leaves, plus more for topping
- 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro (or basil), plus more for topping
- 3 scallions, thinly sliced (white/light green parts separated from dark green parts)
directions:
- Heat a large skillet over medium-high. Add the olive oil and sliced red onion. Cook until the onions are soft and deeply browned, about 7 to 10 minutes.
- Crumble the ground turkey into the skillet and season with a generous pinch of salt. Use a wooden spoon to break up the meat, cooking until it is golden and crisp.
- While the turkey cooks, whisk together the lime juice, fish sauce, red-pepper flakes, torn mint leaves, chopped cilantro, and the white and light green parts of the scallions in a small bowl.
- Pour the prepared sauce into the skillet with the browned turkey. Add the sugar snap peas and toss everything to combine. Cover the skillet and let the peas steam until tender and cooked through, about 3 to 5 minutes.
- Uncover and taste. Add another splash of fish sauce, lime juice, or a pinch of salt if needed. Stir in the chopped cashews or peanuts if using.
- Remove from the heat and top generously with extra torn mint, cilantro, and the reserved dark green scallion slices. Serve immediately.
---

Inspired by the bright, zesty flavors of Thai larb, this one-pan dinner uses fish sauce and lime juice to completely transform unassuming ground turkey. Don't skip the fresh herbs—they are essential to the dish's vibrant flavor.
