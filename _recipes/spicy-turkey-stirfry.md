title: "Spicy Turkey Stir-Fry with Crisp Garlic and Ginger"

servings: 4
prep_time: 10 mins
cook_time: 10 mins

ingredients:
- 1 lb ground turkey (or finely chopped turkey breast)
- 3 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
- 6 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- 1 (2-inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and julienned
- 2–3 scallions, white and green parts separated and sliced
- 1–2 fresh red chilies or 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
- 3 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
- 1 tablespoon oyster sauce or hoisin (optional)
- 1 teaspoon sesame oil
- 1 teaspoon granulated sugar or honey
- 1 teaspoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons water (slurry)
- Salt and black pepper, to taste
- Cooked rice or noodles, for serving
- Sesame seeds, for garnish (optional)

directions:
- Whisk soy sauce, rice vinegar, oyster sauce (if using), sesame oil, sugar, and cornstarch slurry in a small bowl; set aside.
- Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add sliced garlic in a single layer and cook, stirring, until just golden and crisp; transfer to a paper-towel-lined plate.
- Increase heat to high and add remaining oil. Add ground turkey in a thin layer; let brown without stirring for 1–2 minutes, then break up and continue to cook until mostly cooked through and starting to brown.
- Push turkey to one side. Add ginger, white parts of the scallions, and chilies; stir-fry until fragrant, about 30–60 seconds.
- Pour sauce over the turkey and stir vigorously to coat. Cook until sauce thickens and glazes the meat, 1–2 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
- Stir in most of the crisped garlic and the green scallion slices. Remove from heat and drizzle with a little extra sesame oil if desired.
- Serve immediately over rice or noodles and sprinkle with remaining garlic and sesame seeds.


---

notes:
- For extra crisp garlic, watch closely — it can burn quickly. Slice thin and fry on medium heat.
- Swap ground turkey for ground chicken, pork, or firm tofu.
- Make a double batch of sauce and keep leftovers refrigerated up to 3 days; reheat and toss with freshly cooked grains and vegetables.
