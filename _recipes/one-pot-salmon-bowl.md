---
layout: recipe
title: "One-Pot Salmon, Spinach and Lentil Salad"
image: one-pot-salmon-bowl.jpg
ingredients:
  - 4 (6-ounce) skin-on salmon fillets
  - Kosher salt and black pepper
  - 4 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, divided
  - 1 bunch scallions, thinly sliced (whites and greens separated)
  - 1 pint cherry or grape tomatoes, halved
  - 2 (15-ounce) cans lentils, rinsed and drained
  - 1 (5-ounce) package baby spinach
  - 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  - 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
directions:
  - Pat the salmon fillets dry with paper towels and season generously with salt and pepper.
  - In a large high-sided skillet or Dutch oven, heat 2 tablespoons of the olive oil over medium-high heat.
  - Add the salmon, skin-side down, and cook until the skin is golden and crisp, about 4 to 5 minutes. Flip carefully and cook on the other side until just opaque and cooked through, about 1 to 3 minutes more. Transfer salmon to a plate and set aside.
  - Reduce the heat to medium. Add the remaining 2 tablespoons olive oil to the skillet.
  - Add the scallion whites and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened, about 1 minute.
  - Add the cherry tomatoes and cook, stirring occasionally, until they begin to blister and release their juices, about 3 minutes.
  - Stir in the drained lentils and cook until warmed through, about 2 minutes.
  - Add the spinach in batches, stirring constantly, until just wilted, about 1 to 2 minutes.
  - Remove the pan from the heat. Stir in the lemon juice, Dijon mustard, and scallion greens. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  - Flake the salmon into large chunks and return to the pot (or serve the fillets whole on top of the lentil salad). Serve warm.
---
